What is BAP?

The Buyer Abuse Prevention (BAP) team operates a sophisticated infrastructure managing 40+ evaluation use cases, 11 data streams, 15+ systems, and hundreds of machine learning models. We successfully handled approximately 30 million investigations and 12 million enforcement actions in 2022, preventing over $405 million in fraud losses, and continues to grow with the increasing use of GenAI by sophisticated abusers.

Our Core Focus: BAP is primarily focused on preventing abuse across Amazon's retail ecosystem, where we excel at operating a comprehensive web of data aggregation and real-time risk evaluations across the entire customer journey. We ingest and enrich multiple core system event streams (OWEN for orders, CROW for refunds) to build comprehensive risk profiles incorporating order, return, concession, and refund data across customers and products.

Comprehensive Abuse Definitions & Detection

BAP addresses sophisticated abuse patterns across multiple vectors:

AFN Abuse Types

  • DNR (Delivered not Received): False claims of non-delivery despite tracking confirmation
  • MDR (Materially Different Returns): Returning different items than ordered
  • NSR (Non-Sellable Returns): Returning damaged/non-saleable products
  • RR (Return-less Refunds): Abuse of uneconomical/hazmat return policies
  • FLR (Failed Returns): Receiving refunds without returning promised items

MFN Abuse Types

  • NOTR (Not Received): False merchandise not received claims
  • DIFF (Different Item): Claims about receiving wrong items
  • Returns Abuse: Exploitation of merchant fulfillment return policies
  • Linkage Abuse: Related account concession patterns

Investigation & Enforcement Infrastructure

Paragon Investigation Platform: Our investigators use Paragon, a sophisticated investigation platform that provides comprehensive customer data, order history, and abuse pattern analysis. This enables our 1,000+ abuse investigators to make informed enforcement decisions.

Comprehensive Action Portfolio: BAP tooling enables a full spectrum of graduated responses:

  • PFOC (Pre-Fulfillment Order Cancellation): Preventing suspicious orders from shipping
  • RFS (Refund at First Scan Suppression): Blocking automatic refund processes
  • IDV (Identity Verification): Requiring additional customer verification
  • Solicit: Requesting additional information from customers
  • Warn: Issuing policy violation warnings
  • Account Closure: Permanent account termination for egregious abuse
  • Payment Suppression: Blocking payment method usage
  • Police Report Frictions: Requiring law enforcement documentation

Core Capabilities

BAP's intelligence is powered by sophisticated machine learning models trained on massive datasets to identify and predict abusive behavior patterns with unprecedented accuracy.

BEARS Score

Buyer Enforcements Abuse Risk Score - A sophisticated ML-based account-level abuse risk scoring model operating on a 0-100 scale. High precision, low recall approach ideal for targeted frictions.

DWARS

DoorWay to Access Risk - Provides structured risk evaluation decisions through Amazon's Decision Workflow, encapsulating assessments into actionable decisions rather than raw scores.

ATS

Amazon Trust Score - Focuses on identifying trustworthy customers using rank aggregation methodology, considering spending patterns, tenure, Prime status, and economic value.

Automated Actions

Pre-fulfillment order cancellation, account suspension, identity verification, refund suppression, and manual investigator review capabilities.
